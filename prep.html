Lesson 2: Prepositions

What are prepositions?

Prepositions are small but important words used to connect elements in a sentence. They show relationships between words, such as location, direction, time, cause, and manner. In Portuguese, prepositions are often combined with other words, forming contractions (e.g., em + a = na).

Here are some common types of prepositions in Portuguese:

  • Prepositions of Place: Show where something is located.
    • em (in/on): A chave está na mesa. (The key is on the table.)
    • sobre (on/over): O livro está sobre a cadeira. (The book is on the chair.)
    • dentro de (inside): A comida está dentro da geladeira. (The food is inside the fridge.)
  • Prepositions of Direction/Movement: Show where something is going or moving.
    • para (to): Eu vou para a escola. (I am going to school.)
    • até (until/to): Ele foi até a praia. (He went to the beach.)
    • de (from): Ela voltou de Paris. (She returned from Paris.)
  • Prepositions of Time: Indicate when something happens.
    • em (in/on): Nós viajamos em janeiro. (We traveled in January.)
    • desde (since): Ela trabalha aqui desde 2010. (She has worked here since 2010.)
    • durante (during): Estudamos durante a manhã. (We study during the morning.)

Examples

1. O livro está na mesa.

(The book is on the table.)

na (em + a) = on the | mesa = table

2. Eu vou para a escola.

(I am going to school.)

para = to | escola = school

3. Nós viajamos de carro.

(We traveled by car.)

de = by | carro = car

4. Ele mora em São Paulo.

(He lives in São Paulo.)

em = in

5. A comida está dentro da geladeira.

(The food is inside the refrigerator.)

dentro = inside | da (de + a) = of the | geladeira = refrigerator

6. Nós estamos sobre a ponte.

(We are on the bridge.)

sobre = on/above | ponte = bridge

7. Ela saiu com os amigos.

(She went out with her friends.)

com = with | os amigos = her friends

8. Ele veio de bicicleta.

(He came by bike.)

de = by | bicicleta = bike

9. O filme começa às oito horas.

(The movie starts at eight o'clock.)

às (a + as) = at | oito horas = eight o'clock

10. Eu trabalho desde as sete da manhã.

(I have been working since seven in the morning.)

desde = since | as sete = seven o'clock | da manhã = in the morning
